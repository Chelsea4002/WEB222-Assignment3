Who is DK?

DK (Dokyeom) is the main vocalist of K-POP (Korean Popular Music) boy group SEVENTEEN, the leader of Seventeen's mix unit BSS, and a musical actor.
He uses his stage name Dokeyom, which means trying many things, instead of his real name Seok-min Lee, which means becoming a big person like a jade stone.
He was born in Seoul, capital of South Korea on February 18, 1997. His family members are mom, dad, an older sister, and a dog named Wooju.

He is known as having a voice of great volume within a group. As a proof of the fact, other members said that when he sang in the basement, they heard the sound from the CEO's office on the 4th floor of the company building. Members of other K-pop groups also mentioned that they could hear his voice in another room of broadcasting station.

He is also known as a big fan lover. He always expresses his appreciation for the fans who like him and his love for the fans. He tries to communicate with his fans as much as possible through Weverse which is a fan community platform, cheer for them by encouraging them with some warm and powerful words and spread happiness to them.


How did he become a singer?

When he was in middle school, he was very impressed by the stage of YB, a Korean rock band, on the TV program I am a Singer, a Korean music competition, and thought, I want to give that impression and energy to people like them. With that thought, he dreamed of becoming a singer.

He's been showing his respect and love for his role model YB by posting cover videos of their songs I'm a Butterfly, Love to, Permint Candy and Blue Whale.

At the recommendation of the director of the practical music academy he attended for his dream, he took an audition held by Pledis entertainment in Korea and was notified of his acceptance. He joined Pledis Entertainment in 2012 and made his debut as SEVENTEEN on May 26, 2015, after about three years of practice.


Hobbies

According to his resume that he wrote to show his fans, his hobbies are playing guitar, singing with earphones on when resting, and watching movies.


Activities

Pre-debut

When he was a trainee, he performed as a stage backup dancer for K-pop groups of the same Entertainment agency, After School, NU'EST, Hello Venus, and Orange Caramel.

After debut

  • He was recognized for his singing skills by performing on the Masked Singer program, a Korean music show that is evaluated by the audience by singing on stage wearing a specially designed mask.
  • He continued his individual activities as well as Seventeen group activities, including the release of the duet song Cinematic Love with Korean ballad singer Yang Da-il and collaboration song 17 with R&B singer Pink Sweat$ and the release of Missed Connections, Is it Still Beautiful and Go! as Korean drama OST (Original Sound Track) songs.
  • He also made his debut as a musical actor through the musical Excalibur. He played Arthur, one of the main characters in the musical.
  • In addition, he repays his love to fans by posting cover videos of various songs such as She Didn't Love Me by Yang Da-il, the Road by Paul Kim, Amusement Park by Baekhyun, and Betelgeuse by Yuuri.

  • BSS

    • BSS (Boo Seok Soon) is a mix unit made up of three members in SEVENTEEN: Seungkwan, Dokyeom, and Hoshi. The group's name is a combination of their real names (Boo from Seungkwan Boo, Seok from Seokmin Lee, Soon from Soonyoung Kwon).
